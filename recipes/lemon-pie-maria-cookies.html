Lemon Pie with Maria Cookies

Description

Lemon Pie with Maria Cookies is a refreshing and sweet dessert that's simple to make and perfect for any occasion. The crust is made with Maria cookies, a popular type of biscuit, which adds a subtle sweetness and a crunchy texture.

The filling is a creamy and tangy lemon mixture that balances the sweetness of the crust. Itâ€™s a no-bake dessert, making it a convenient option for a quick yet delicious treat.

Ingredients

  • 1 package of Maria cookies (about 200g)
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 can (395g) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1/2 cup freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
  • 1 cup heavy cream (whipped)
  • Fresh lemon slices for garnish (optional)

Steps

  1. Prepare the crust: Crush the Maria cookies into fine crumbs using a food processor or a rolling pin. Mix the crumbs with the melted butter until everything is well coated. Press the mixture into the bottom of a pie dish to form an even crust. Chill in the refrigerator for 30 minutes to set.
  2. Make the filling: In a mixing bowl, combine the sweetened condensed milk, lemon juice, and lemon zest. Stir until smooth.
  3. Whip the cream: In a separate bowl, whip the heavy cream until stiff peaks form. Gently fold the whipped cream into the lemon mixture.
  4. Assemble the pie: Once the crust is set, pour the lemon filling over the chilled crust and smooth the top with a spatula.
  5. Chill the pie: Refrigerate the pie for at least 3 hours, or until the filling is firm.
  6. Serve: Garnish with fresh lemon slices if desired, and serve cold.
