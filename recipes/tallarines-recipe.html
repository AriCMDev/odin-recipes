Tallarines

Description

Tallarines (noodles) are a popular dish in many cultures, often served with a variety of sauces, meats, or vegetables. This simple yet delicious recipe features soft noodles tossed in a flavorful sauce that can be customized based on your preferences.

Whether served as a side dish or a main course, tallarines are a versatile option that can be paired with just about anything!

Ingredients

  • 250g tallarines (egg noodles or pasta)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or butter
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh herbs (parsley or basil), chopped (optional)
  • Grated Parmesan cheese (optional)
  1. Cook the noodles: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the tallarines (noodles) according to the package instructions, usually for 8-10 minutes, until tender. Drain and set aside.
  2. Prepare the sauce: In a large pan, heat the olive oil or butter over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and saut√© for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Add broth and soy sauce: Pour in the chicken or vegetable broth and soy sauce. Stir to combine, letting the sauce simmer for 2-3 minutes.
  4. Toss the noodles: Add the cooked noodles to the pan, tossing them gently in the sauce to coat them evenly. Add black pepper and salt to taste.
  5. Serve: Garnish with fresh herbs and grated Parmesan cheese if desired. Serve hot.
